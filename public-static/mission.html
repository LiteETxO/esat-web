Who We Are

Journalism in the service of truth

Ethiopian Satellite Television was founded on a single conviction: that Ethiopians — at home and in the diaspora — deserve free, fearless, and accountable journalism.

Founded April 24, 2010

"We were founded in exile because the truth had no home inside Ethiopia. We return to Addis Ababa because the truth always finds its way home."

ESAT Editorial Board · Washington D.C., 2010

Our Core Principles

01

Independence

ESAT accepts no funding from any government, political party, or interest group. Our editorial decisions are made by journalists — no one else. Our independence is not a policy; it is a covenant with our audience.

02

Courage

We cover what others won't. We name what others leave unnamed. From conflict zones in Tigray to the corridors of the African Union, ESAT journalists go where the story demands — regardless of risk.

03

Accountability

We hold power to account — not only the power of governments, but of institutions, media organisations, and ourselves. When we get it wrong, we say so. Corrections are published openly and without delay.

04

Plurality

Ethiopia is a nation of more than 80 languages and countless stories. ESAT broadcasts in Amharic, Afaan Oromoo, Tigrigna, and English — because no single voice can speak for a nation this diverse.

Our Story

From exile to Addis Ababa — sixteen years of independent broadcasting

ESAT was established on April 24, 2010, by a group of Ethiopian journalists and activists operating from Washington D.C. and Amsterdam. The founding came at a moment when independent voices inside Ethiopia faced arrest, intimidation, and forced silence. The satellite signal became a lifeline — a way to reach millions of Ethiopians who had no access to uncensored news.

Through a decade of broadcasting from abroad, ESAT reported on elections, famines, protests, and political upheaval. When the political landscape shifted in 2018, ESAT's journalists began returning to Addis Ababa. The satellite station became a terrestrial broadcaster — not by abandoning its founding values, but by proving them.

Today ESAT operates studios in Addis Ababa, Washington D.C., Amsterdam, and London. We reach more than four million viewers weekly across television, radio, and digital platforms. We are funded entirely by the Ethiopian public — through subscriptions, donations, and diaspora partnerships — with no government money accepted, ever.

What We Commit To

  • ✦

    No government funding — ever

    ESAT has never accepted a single birr, dollar, or euro from any government. We are funded by our audience. That is the only way a free press can remain free.

  • ✦

    Transparent corrections

    Errors are corrected promptly and published openly. We do not silently edit stories. Every correction names the mistake, the correction, and the date it was made.

  • ✦

    Source protection

    We will never reveal a source without explicit consent. Journalists who report for ESAT operate under strict protocols to protect those who speak to us at personal risk.

  • ✦

    Multilingual reach

    We are committed to reporting in Ethiopia's major languages. Amharic and Afaan Oromoo broadcasts receive equal editorial resources. Tigrigna and English services continue to grow.

  • ✦

    Press freedom advocacy

    ESAT documents threats to journalists across Africa and advocates publicly for detained reporters. We believe press freedom is not a professional interest — it is a human right.
